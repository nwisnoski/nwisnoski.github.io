I am a PhD student in the Lennon Lab at Indiana University studying microbial ecology. I am particularly interested in spatial patterns of biodiversity within stream networks, the implications of dormancy & dispersal for metacommunity dynamics, and the constraint imposed by ecosystem residence time.

Hyporheic microbial ecology

The hyporheic zone of a stream is the interface between its surface and ground waters. Hyporheic zones contain rich microbial communities that regulate whole-stream biogeochemistry.

Dormancy and dispersal

Dormancy is a bet-hedging strategy of many microbial taxa by which cells enter a reversible state of metabolic inactivity. Dormant microbes contribute to the maintenance of biodiversity by "re-seeding" the active microbial community when favorable environmental conditions permit. While dormancy provides an avenue for temporal maintenance of diversity, dispersal, the movement of individuals, maintains diversity spatially.

Dendritic metacommunities

Metacommunities are local communities linked by dispersal. Understanding how dispersal maintains local and regional diversity within a metacommunity has been well characterized in lattice networks. Dendritic networks are hierarchical and branching in structure, which constrains dispersal rate and directionality.
